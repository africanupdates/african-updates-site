Former tennis player and music giant, Frenchman Yannick Noah, is confident that the 'ATP Challenger 50 Tour' tournament is being held in Rwanda, which is an opportunity for young people that he has not seen. He announced this in his first interview with the media at Heaven Restaurant & Boutique Hotel on the morning of Thursday, February 29, 2024. The head of the Rwanda Tennis Association, FRT, Karenzi Théoneste, said that participating in important tournaments like the one held in Rwanda by Yannick Noah, has a great contribution in attracting young people to the game.

This was confirmed by the President of the African Tennis Association (CAT), Jean-Claude Talon, who was happy that the tournament was held in Rwanda. “I'm happy to be here at this historic tournament. The fact that the management of Rwanda and Rwanda Tennis has agreed to host this tournament, we are very grateful. Yes, we are still a long way off, but when you can welcome players like Yannick Noah, it gives us hope that the beginning is good."

Noah “I was at home last week when they told me that I will be going to a good place. I felt that there is no good country like Cameroon but I found this is a good place to stay, they welcomed us very well.” “It's a surprise to me. As a tennis player I have never had the opportunity to play at home in Cameroon except for regular friendly matches. When I hear a competition like this it touches my heart. I think it's about getting kids to see people who play, and they'll become players because it's as big a game as any other.

The manager of the ATP Challenger Tour, Eric Lamquet, said that the reason why Rwanda was chosen to host the tournament is that "it has a story to tell the world, a story that would help unite people. We as organizers of the tournament are not only looking to promote Tennis, but also to promote the community as a whole.” The "ATP Challenger 50 Tour" tournament is being held in Rwanda from February 26 to March 10, 2024.
