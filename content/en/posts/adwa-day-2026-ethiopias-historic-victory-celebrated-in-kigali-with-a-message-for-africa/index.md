As Ethiopia marks Adwa Day on March 2, 2026, the spirit of African unity and resilience echoed strongly in Kigali, where the 130th Anniversary of the Victory of Adwa was commemorated over the weekend.

On Saturday, February 28, the Embassy of Ethiopia in Kigali, in collaboration with the Ethiopian Community in Rwanda, hosted a vibrant celebration that blended history, environmental action, and cultural pride. The event began in the morning with participation in Umuganda, Rwanda’s nationally recognized community service initiative. Embassy staff and community members joined local residents in cleaning and tree planting, sending a strong message about shared responsibility and sustainability.

> \[penci\_blockquote style="style-3" align="left" author="" font\_style="italic" uppercase="false" text\_size="12"\]\[/penci\_blockquote\] At a time when much of Africa was falling under colonial rule, Ethiopia’s decisive triumph stood as a beacon of hope across the continent. Adwa became more than a military victory; it became a symbol of African dignity, resistance, and independence.

Speaking during the commemoration, Ethiopia’s Ambassador underscored that the unity which secured victory at Adwa is the same unity needed today to confront global challenges, particularly climate change. He highlighted Ethiopia’s Green Legacy Initiative, launched in 2019 under Prime Minister Abiy Ahmed, through which more than 40 billion trees have been planted. The initiative has positioned Ethiopia as a continental leader in environmental protection and climate action.

Officials from the City of Kigali welcomed the Ethiopian community’s participation in Umuganda, praising the strong cooperation between Rwanda and Ethiopia. The event also featured a proposal to establish a green park named “Adwa,” symbolizing shared African resilience and solidarity.



Later in the evening, the formal program gave way to celebration. Members of the diplomatic corps, professionals, business leaders, and community members gathered to honor the historic day in a warm and festive atmosphere. Guests enjoyed traditional Ethiopian cuisine and cultural exchanges that reflected the richness of Ethiopia’s heritage and the strength of its ties with Rwanda. The celebration was not only about remembering the past, but also about strengthening bonds between African nations.

For a continent still shaping its development path, Adwa continues to carry deep meaning. It reminds Africa that unity can overcome adversity and that sovereignty and dignity are worth defending. As environmental pressures and global uncertainties grow, the message of Adwa resonates beyond Ethiopia’s borders.

One hundred and thirty years later, the victory remains a continental milestone. In Kigali this weekend, history met the present, and the enduring spirit of Adwa once again affirmed that Africa’s strength lies in its unity.
